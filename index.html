About Me

I am currently a master's student in Computer Technology at Shenzhen University. My research focuses on federated learning, edge intelligence, mobile edge computing, and efficient distributed AI systems. I am particularly interested in decentralized federated learning, energy-efficient client selection, resource allocation, and system optimization for resource-constrained edge devices.

I received my B.Eng. degree in Computer Science and Technology from the University of Science and Technology Beijing.

Research Experience

Energy-Efficient Federated Learning in Mobile Edge Computing via Fine-Grained Energy Planning Client Selection
Guangdong Laboratory of Artificial Intelligence and Digital Economy & Shenzhen University, 2025–2026

  • Proposed an energy-aware client selection strategy for federated learning in mobile edge computing environments.
  • Dynamically adjusted local training workloads according to residual device energy.
  • Estimated transmission energy consumption using historical channel information.

Fast-DFL: Flexible Client Switching for Bandwidth-Constrained Hierarchical Decentralized Federated Learning
Master's thesis research, Shenzhen University, 2023–present

  • Designed an asynchronous two-level decentralized federated learning framework to reduce idle waiting time and staleness.
  • Developed a proactive server selection mechanism based on real-time channel conditions.
  • Applied priority-based bandwidth allocation and extra local training to improve system efficiency.
  • Introduced a threshold-based push-pull consensus protocol to accelerate knowledge propagation among edge servers.

Federated Learning in Dynamic Mobile Scenarios
Master's thesis research, Shenzhen University, 2023–present

  • Studied federated learning under client mobility and limited energy constraints.
  • Modeled user mobility using real-world datasets and transition matrices.
  • Designed mobility-aware server communication thresholds and energy-aware client selection mechanisms.
